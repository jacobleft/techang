# Relationship Building Blocks

This document provides scientific insights into the formation and fundamental dynamics of intimate relationships.

## The Nature of Intimacy
Intimate relationships differ from casual associations in seven ways: knowledge, interdependence, caring, trust, responsiveness, mutuality, and commitment. The most important of these is **responsiveness**—the perception that our partners recognize, understand, and support our needs.

## Attraction
- **Proximity**: We like those who are physically close to us. Familiarity breeds attraction.
- **Physical Attractiveness**: We are drawn to beauty, but tend to pair off with those of similar attractiveness (matching hypothesis).
- **Reciprocity**: We like those who like us. Playing hard to get is less effective than being selectively eager.
- **Similarity**: Opposites rarely attract long-term. We prefer those who share our backgrounds, values, and attitudes.

## Social Cognition
- **First Impressions**: They matter immensely and linger due to confirmation bias.
- **Positive Illusions**: Happy partners idealize each other, emphasizing strengths and minimizing flaws.
- **Attributions**: Happy couples make relationship-enhancing attributions (giving credit for good behavior and excusing bad behavior as an accident). Unhappy couples do the reverse.

## Interdependency
- Relationships are an economic exchange. We evaluate outcomes against our expectations (Comparison Level) and alternatives (Comparison Level for Alternatives).
- **Commitment**: The intention to continue the relationship is driven by satisfaction, lack of better alternatives, and high investments.

## Friendship and Love
- **Friendship**: Based on affection, communion, and companionship without the exclusivity or intense passions of romance.
- **Love**: Sternberg's Triangular Theory highlights three components: Intimacy, Passion, and Commitment.
  - *Romantic Love*: Intimacy + Passion
  - *Companionate Love*: Intimacy + Commitment (A profound, friendship-based love that is best for long-term marriage survival).
  - *Fatuous Love*: Passion + Commitment
  - *Consummate Love*: Intimacy + Passion + Commitment

## Sexuality
- **Communication is Key**: Clear communication about sex is associated with greater satisfaction. Many people rely too heavily on nonverbal cues, leading to misunderstandings.

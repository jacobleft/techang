# Contrast-Revelation Audit

## Use when

Use when prose reveals a rejected frame, hidden anxiety, prompt history, reviewer pressure, or authorial defensiveness through negation, contrast, disclaimer, or over-explanation.

Typical triggers:

- “don’t mention X” / “avoid X” / “remove X”
- “this sounds defensive”
- “此地无银”
- “too much not X but Y”
- “the reader will suspect something”
- “remove traces of the previous version”
- “why does this feel like protesting too much?”

## Failure mode

The prose tries to remove or distinguish itself from X, but by naming X it makes X salient. The reader receives the rejected frame instead of the intended positive picture.

## Lenses

### 1. Local defensive negation

Unit: phrase or sentence.

Look for:

- `not X but Y`
- `not merely`, `not just`, `rather than`, `instead of`, `unlike`, `in contrast to`
- `this does not mean`, `we do not claim`, `we do not intend`
- `并非`, `不是……而是`, `并不意味着`, `并不是说`, `区别于`, `不同于`, `不能简单理解为`

Question: is this contrast necessary for interpretation, or only reassurance?

Keep a contrast only when all four conditions hold:

1. The reader must distinguish two live concepts.
2. Both sides of the contrast are defined or immediately understandable.
3. The contrast appears at the right structural level, usually once.
4. The positive frame can stand independently afterward.

### 2. Self-revealing slip

Unit: sentence function / authorial posture.

Look for sentences that reveal anxiety or revision history:

- “It should be noted that...”
- “Although it may appear...”
- “This does not imply...”
- “这里需要说明的是……”
- “容易误解为……”

Question: what hidden worry does this sentence reveal?

### 3. Whole-picture contrast

Unit: paragraph, section, or whole document.

Look for a text whose identity depends on what it rejects:

- repeated contrasts against the same ghost frame
- contribution defined by negating alternatives
- section headings organized around denial
- positive frame unable to stand without enemy vocabulary

Question: does the positive picture stand on its own?

## Artifact

Produce this table when diagnosing:

```text
Trace | Ghost frame | Scale | Reader effect | Action | Positive replacement
```

Scale is one of: phrase, sentence, paragraph, section, whole document.

## Repair operations

1. Delete the trace if it only reassures.
2. If the concern is real, convert it into a reader-facing boundary or definition.
3. Replace negative identity with positive mechanism.
4. Keep at most one necessary research-gap contrast; remove repeated local denials.
5. Rename headings around the positive frame, not the rejected frame.

## Examples

Bad:

> This is not a perching mechanism, but a compliant contact strategy.

Diagnosis:

- Trace: “not a perching mechanism, but”
- Ghost frame: perching
- Reader effect: makes the reader wonder why perching is being denied

Good:

> The mechanism enables compliant contact with slender structures through controllable stiffness and load-transfer paths.

Chinese bad:

> 本文并非模仿鸟类栖附行为，而是研究张拉整体结构的接触稳定机制。

Chinese good:

> 本文研究螺线张拉整体单元在复杂接触条件下的构型稳定、刚度调控与力传递机制。

## Tractatus grounding

Silence is an operation: what should not enter the reader’s picture should not reappear as denial. When a boundary can be shown by clean positive structure, do not say it defensively.

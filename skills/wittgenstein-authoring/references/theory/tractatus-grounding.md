# Tractatus Grounding for Authoring

This file explains why the operational audits exist. Do not lead with this theory in ordinary user-facing output.

## Silence

Authoring operation: do not state what the document cannot responsibly say. Silence can mean omission, narrowing, moving to future work, or marking an open issue.

Operational home:

- Exposition Boundary Audit
- Contrast-Revelation Audit when a rejected frame should disappear entirely

## Saying vs Showing

Authoring operation: say what the reader needs explicitly; show relations, priorities, rigor, and structure through form, examples, order, and omission.

Operational home:

- Structural Skeleton Audit
- Improve Agent Skill Prose workflow

## Logical syntax / sign-symbol-use

Authoring operation: each visible term must have a stable role in context. A sign without a role is noise; a sign with shifting roles is confusion.

Operational home:

- Term and Naming Discipline Audit

## Ladder / scaffolding

Authoring operation: support reader ascent with definitions, examples, bridges, and staged structure when direct statement would be unintelligible. Remove scaffolding only when it becomes obstructive.

Operational home:

- Structural Skeleton Audit, especially ladder/progression check

## Picture / model

Authoring operation: prose, examples, diagrams, and models should preserve the relevant structure of what they represent. Do not replace structure with vibes or claims of rigor.

Operational home:

- Structural Skeleton Audit
- Term and Naming Discipline Audit
- Translate Source to Reader-Facing Prose workflow

## Compression

The whole skill can be summarized as:

```text
Reader-facing prose discipline
├── leakage control: Contrast-Revelation
├── naming control: Term Discipline
├── structure control: Structural Skeleton
└── scope control: Exposition Boundary
```
